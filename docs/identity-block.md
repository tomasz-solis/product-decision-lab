# Identity and copy deck

The point of this file is to make my level and focus clear in the first few
seconds someone spends on the lab, on LinkedIn, or on a CV. Every line leads with
the decision the work improves, not the method behind it.

One deliberate split: the lab is where I stand out, so it leads with the niche
title. The CV and LinkedIn play it safer with more searchable titles, because
those surfaces get filtered by recruiters and keyword tools.

## Positioning

- **Lab / portfolio:** Product Decision Scientist.
- **CV / LinkedIn:** Senior Product Data Scientist, with a specialization line —
  Decision Science, Experimentation, Causal Inference. Keep "Analyst" reachable in
  the body text for the many EU and remote roles still titled that way.
- Systems thinking is the through-line. I show it with the work; it is never the
  title.

Why Product Decision Scientist and not just Data Scientist: it describes applying
quantitative methods to decisions, which is what I actually do, without implying
production ML I don't. It is the more accurate title and the more distinctive one.

## The NDA framing (use it the same way everywhere)

Say this plainly on each headline app and once on the lab page. It explains why the
public work looks like a clean, generalized engine rather than a real deployment:

> This is the reusable engine. In practice it runs against real OKRs, telemetry, and
> stakeholders I can't publish. What's public is the generalized version — and
> generalizing it well took more judgment than the original build.

It does two things at once: it tells the reader they're looking at the residue of real
work, and it makes the case that the generalization itself is the harder skill — without
naming an employer.
